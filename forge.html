Forge Platform

Local-first applications with structured workflows and optional integration.

Forge is the public platform language for BDS-built applications: disciplined desktop software with clear boundaries, deliberate operator control, and the option to connect outward when that actually improves the workflow.

Local-first Structured workflows Optional integration

Platform pillars

The public explanation stays intentionally high level. It describes the user-facing operating model, not internal service topology.

Controlled local baseline

Core work can remain on the user’s machine instead of assuming every action belongs in a remote browser session.

Structured workflow surfaces

Interfaces are designed around real sequences of work, not novelty interactions that create drift and confusion.

Optional ecosystem support

Specialized services can be added when they improve validation, evidence, or automation, but they are not the baseline identity of the product.

Governed expansion

When an application connects outward, those connections are deliberate and bounded rather than ad hoc.

SMITH preview

SMITH is the workflow-intelligence layer used inside Forge applications. It is calm, assistive, and intentionally not a generic chatbot overlay.

Meet SMITH

SMITH stays small, visible, and subordinate to the user. It does not auto-open, does not interrupt the operator, and does not ask to run the room.

Meet SMITH

Optional ecosystem connectivity

The ecosystem exists to amplify certain workflows, not to drag every product into permanent cloud dependence.

When connected

Applications can reach specialized services for validation, AI orchestration, evidence assembly, or agent-style execution when those services materially improve the outcome.

When disconnected

The public posture remains local-first. Products should remain understandable and useful even when the ecosystem lane is not active.

Why structure matters

Structure is not branding garnish. It is how products stay trustworthy when the work becomes complex.

Deliberate interfaces age better

Products built around authority, clarity, and recoverability can support serious work longer than products designed to impress for five minutes.
