Why AuthorForge Exists

Founder context matters here because the product is solving a workflow problem that only becomes obvious when you have actually tried to publish books through fragmented tooling.

How it works

The flow is simple on purpose: build the story system, refine with discipline, then move through a deliberate distribution stage instead of improvising at the end.

Step 01
Build the Story System
Start with manuscript structure, series scaffolding, characters, lore, and geography so the narrative has a governed foundation before drafting accelerates.
Step 02
Refine with Discipline
Run revision passes against canon, arcs, continuity, and scene intent so changes stay coherent instead of creating new drift elsewhere in the book.
Step 03
Prepare for Distribution
Finalize packaging, validate trim and bleed, confirm publishing readiness, and export into distributor-ready formats without leaving the AuthorForge workflow.

FAQ

Use native disclosure controls so the answers stay accessible, indexable, and script-free.

Do I need Pro to publish a book?

No. Standard is intended to include the full Authoring OS for writing and publishing. Pro adds cloud AI workflows, not the basic ability to ship a book.

Is AuthorForge cloud-based?

No. The core product is local-first and offline functional. Pro introduces optional cloud-backed AI services, but the product is not designed around mandatory cloud dependence.

Does AuthorForge replace cover or formatting tools?

It aims to replace much of the fragmented author stack by bringing cover prep, spine-width calculation, publishing validation, and deterministic export into one workflow. Some external tools may still remain in edge cases, but AuthorForge is designed to reduce that sprawl.
